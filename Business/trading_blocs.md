# 4.1.5 Trading blocs

- A trading bloc is a group of countries that form an agreement to reduce or eliminate protectionnist measures (tariffs and 
  quotas) between each others.
- Joining a trading bloc is a key method of increasing trade.
- Depending on how closely the members wish to integrate their economies they may form different types of trading blocs such as;
  free trade areas, custom unions, common markets and full economies & monetary union.

## Expansion of trade blocs:
- Expansion of trading bloc; the process of more countries joining an existing trading bloc, thereby making it expand.
- Joining a trading block is a key method of increasing trade liberalisation and leads to trade creation.
- Trade creation means that businesses are able to enter new markets, which can lead to an increase in sales volume and sales 
  volume.

## What are the benefits of expansion of trading blocs?
- Access to larger markets
- Economies of scale by producing more and selling more
- Enhanced competition
- Migration with a good supply of able bodies workers

## Three of the largest trading blocs include:
- The European Union (EU)
- The Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN)
- USMCA (US, Mexico and Canada, formely known as NAFTA)


The EU is an economic union, originally formed in 1993.
It currently has 27 member states that are located in Europe.
The union has an estimated population of 450 million people. 
EU policies aim to ensure that free movement of people, 
goods, services and capital within the internal market.
Includes: Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, France, Ireland, 
Italy, Austria, Sweden...

ASEAN is a regional grouping of all 11 states in Southeeast
Asian, which aims to promote economic and security cooperation
among its members. Together, its member states represent a 
population of more than 600 million. The primary objedtive
of ASEAN is to acceletate economic growth, social progress 
and cultural development in the region, as well as to promote 
regional peace and stability.
Includes: Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Cambodia, Brunei, 
Malaysia, Philipines...


The USMCA is a free trade agreement among the US, Mexico, 
and Canada.
Includes: USA, Mexico, Canada

## Benefits of trade blocs
- Access to huge markets e.g. EU is 447 million potential
  customers.
- Free movement of goods and people between countries in 
  the trading bloc.
- Helps smaller developing nations to reach a wider audience.

## Drawbacks
- Some trade blocs insist that countries cannot be part of 
  another trade bloc, which can limit which nations they 
  sell into.
- Larger or more developed countries often gain more, while 
  smaller or weaker economies may struggle to compete.


# Opportunities
- Free trade is trade that takes place between countries 
  without protectionism:
    - Freedom to trade
    - Enlarged market
    - Protection from international competition outside of 
      the bloc
    - Freedom of movement of people

# Drawbacks of free trade
- Free trade agreements (such as trading blocs) can also 
  create problems for business:
    - Dominance of developed countries in global trading,
    - It can kill off domestic business in developing nations
    - It can reduce national sovereignity or identity as 
      countries become standardised, westernised and 
      'McDonalized'.
