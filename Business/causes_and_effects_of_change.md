# 3.6.1 Causes and effects of change:


## 1. Cause of change: Changes in organisational size:
The business may grow in size for example if it expands 
internationally.
Issuses the business will face as a result could be:
> (a) Maintaining the company culture,
> (b) Motivating staff during the expansion,
> (c) Increased labour costs with hiring of new staff,
> (d) Training of new staff.

## 2. Poor business performance:
This may happpen if the business experiences poor sales, low
profits or slow expansion.
Issues the business will face as a result:
> (a) The business will need new objectives and a new 
> direction,
> (b) The business will need some new strategies to compete
> (Ansoff's),
> (c) The business wil need to look at what is necessary to 
> improve performance; this may mean delayering or 
> redundancies.

## 3. New ownership:
This may happen if the business has been bought or merged 
with another business or there has been a managemenet 
buyout.
Issues the business will face as a result:
> (a) There may be significant role duplication, so there 
> may need to be redundancies,
> (b) There may be a clash of cultures,
> (c) There may be issues of communication between the two 
> business as they change and merge.

## 4. Transformational leadership:
Transformational leadership - the ability to implement a 
vision through radical polices and strategies to bring
about a positive change.
This may happen if new leadership is brought into the 
organisation that seeks to change it.
Issues the business will face as a result:
> (a) The business will need to reinvent itself to achieve 
> competetive advantage,
> (b) A new businesss culture which challenges managers to 
> develop new ways of thinking,
> (c) The business encourages the development of new ideas.

## 5. The market and other external factors (PESTLE):
This may happen if there have been new entrants to the 
market (porter's 5 forces), due to EU expansion or changes 
in the market (e.g. energy market deregulation).
Issues the business will face as a result:
> (a) The business may need to respond by increasing their 
> research and development budget to introduce more 
> innovative products to their portifolio,
> (b) They may need to change the corporate objectives.


# Possible effects of change on a business - Introduction:
All these changes can cause effects on a business, they 
depend on the type, size, and industry, but the general
effects your exam board would like you to know about are:
> 1. Competetiveness
> 2. Productivity
> 3. Financial performance
> 4. Stakeholders (internal)
> 5. Stakeholders (external)

##  1. Effect on change on competetiveness:
A business that is undergoing change may need to:
> (a) Be aware of competitors actions and be prepared to 
> react to them,
> (b) Benchmark with similar businessesto make sure they are
> keeping up,
> (c) Invest in research and development to keep innovating 
> and bringing new products to the market,
> (d) Investigate new and emerging markets e.g BRIC 
> economies (Brazil, Russia, India and china) or expanding 
> trade blocs.

## 2. Productivity:
A business that is undergoing change may need to:
> (a) Invest in new equipment and machinery,
> (b) Change production methods (e.g from batch to flow),
> (c) Change the quality management methods (e.g from 
> quality control to TQM,
> (d) Retain managers so their skills meet the new 
> technologies used.

## 3. Financial performance:
A business that is undergoing change may need to:
> (a) Compare sales forecasts with available production 
> capacity,
> (b) Production floor layout adapted for neccessary 
> increases in production,
> (c) Produce new cash flow forecasts.

## 4. Stakegolders (internal):
Stakeholders may be affected by change:
> (a) Employees may feel unsure about their future and 
> promotion prospects,
> (b) Managers may be worried about duplicate roles and
> redundancies, or possibly see the change as positive with
> new opportunities.

## 5. Stakeholders (external):
Stakeholders may be affected by change:
> (a) Customers be delighted with the new range of products
> and improved quality,
> (b) Suppliers may see the change as an opporutnity to 
> renegotiate old contracts with more favourable terms,
> (c) Shareholders may be concerned tht changes will be 
> expensive and have a negative impact on profit.
