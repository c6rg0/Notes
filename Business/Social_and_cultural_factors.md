# Social and cultural factors

- Cultural factors include; Beliefs, moral values, traditions,
  language, laws held by a country.
- Social factors include; lifestyle, religion(s), economic 
  wealth, family structures, eductation and political systems
  held by a country.

# High context/Low context countries
- High context communication needs:
    - Establish social trust first,
    - Value personal relations and good will,
    - Agreement by general trust,
    - Negotiations slow and ritualistic.
- Low context communication needs:
    - Get down to business first,
    - Value expertise and performance,
    - Agreement by specific, legalistic contract,
    - Negotiations efficient as possible.

# Example of cultural differences
- When Procter and Gamble launched pampers nappies in Japan,
  they printed on the packaging the typical image of a stork
  delivering a baby, but the product did not perform very
  well.
- After some research, the company foudn out that customers 
  were confused by the image of teh stork with the baby, in
  Japanese folklore it is giant floating peaches on a river
  and not storks that deliver the babies.


# Different tastes

## Understanding your market
- Pepsi lost market share in southeast asia when it changed
  its vending machines from deep blue to light blue.
- Light blue is a symbol of death and mouring in southeast 
  asia.

## OREO - different products for different markets
- Oreo has lots of flavour variations of their cookies for 
  their different markets.
- For example, caramel and apple is sold in the USA but not
  in Australia or the UK.
- Another example is the cinnamon bun flavour is sold in the
  USA but not Canada.

## Kitkats

# Language

## Issues with language when trading internationally
- Overcoming international language barriers is key to the 
  success of trading in other countries.
- Companies will need to be able to operate in a range of 
  languages in order to do business globally.
- Language will be used in marketing and providing the right
  level of service to customers.
- This may involve investing in a relationship with a 
  trustworthy third party company to help the business 
  communicate effectively in another language.
