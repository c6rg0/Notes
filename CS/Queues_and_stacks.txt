Queues:
- Last in first out, new items are put in the back of the queue, and the first are taken out.
 
  Types:
- Circular
- Linear
- Priority

  Applications:
- Output waiting to be printed is commonly stored in a queue on a disk.
- Characters typed at a keyboard are held in a queue in a keyboard buffer.

  Operations:
- Push
- Pop
- Peep/top view
- isEmpty
- isFull

- Breadth first search?


Stacks:
- First in first out, new items are put on top of the stack, and the newest item gets taken off.

  Applications:
- Used to hold return addresses when subroutines are called.
- When you use the undo button, the last operation you carried out is popped from the stack and undone.

  Implementation:
- A stack can be implemented as either a static or dynamic data structure.
